# Figure 2

**Figure 2. Residual-risk target construction from held-out acquired k-space.**
(a) For a held-out subset \(\Lambda\), the fixed SSDU reconstruction \(\hat{x}_v\) was projected into the held-out measurement space using \(E_{\Lambda}\hat{x}_v\), and compared with the acquired held-out k-space data \(y_{\Lambda}\). The resulting residual \(r_{\Lambda}=y_{\Lambda}-E_{\Lambda}\hat{x}_v\) was mapped back through a density-compensated adjoint operation \(E_{\Lambda}^{H}W_{\Lambda}r_{\Lambda}\), squared to obtain \(e_{\Lambda}\), and locally pooled to obtain \(\bar{e}_{\Lambda}\). (b) A probe-estimated gain envelope \(\bar{g}_{\Lambda}\), generated using \(K=4\) random probes, normalised the pooled residual energy under the soft support mask \(M_{\mathrm{soft}}\). The resulting map \(z_{\Lambda}\) was robustly scaled by \(Q_{99,\Lambda}\) and log-compressed to produce the residual-risk target \(u_{\Lambda}\). In this study, \(\Lambda\) denoted either \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{risk},v}\) for residual-risk learning or \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{hold},v}\) for independent verification. The log-scaling constant was \(\alpha=10\), the numerical stabiliser was \(\varepsilon=10^{-8}\), and local pooling used a \(16\times16\) window. DCF = density compensation function.
