# Figure 1

**Figure 1. Four-way acquired k-space partition and role-separated verification workflow.**
(a) Schematic of the four-way partition of acquired k-space for volume \(v\), where \(\Omega_v=\Theta_v\cup\Lambda_{\mathrm{rec},v}\cup\Lambda_{\mathrm{risk},v}\cup\Lambda_{\mathrm{hold},v}\). The subsets were pairwise disjoint and were assigned separate methodological roles. The reconstruction input subset \(\Theta_v\) contained the ACS lines, \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{rec},v}\) was used for the SSDU reconstruction loss, \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{risk},v}\) was used for residual-risk learning, and \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{hold},v}\) was reserved for independent verification. (b) Role-separated workflow. Measurements from \(\Theta_v\) were used to reconstruct \(\hat{x}_v\) and to form the zero-filled reference \(x_{0,v}\). These image-derived quantities formed the A4 tensor \(C_v\), which was passed to the residual-risk predictor. The residual-risk target from \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{risk},v}\) supervised the predictor, whereas \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{hold},v}\) was used only for independent verification with metrics, bootstrap analysis, and conformal calibration. ACS = autocalibration signal; SSDU = self-supervised learning via data undersampling.
